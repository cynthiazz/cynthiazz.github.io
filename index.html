Cynthia Zhong

钟心悦 ['sɪnθiə d̥ʒʊŋ]

Hello! I am a student at UC Berkeley majoring in Linguistics and Computer Science. I grew up in Beijing, China, and my family moved to Palo Alto, California when I was 13. I have always found languages deeply fascinating and can never get tired learning more about them. In particular, the intimate ties between human language and modern machines blow me away and make me excited to explore the intersection of these two fields.

In my free time, I like to play (video game soundtracks on) the piano, arrange music, read, and spend hours walking around in big cities while having my playlist on loop.

Lastly, feel free to contact me at cynthia.zz@berkeley.edu!

Research

Tone in Mandarin-English Code Switching

August 2019 - present

Research Assistant

Together with my partner, we recruited 32 English-Mandarin bilingual participants and ran experiments with them in the Berkeley PhonLab. More than 2000 segments of audio data were collected by the end of the semester, which I annotated using Praat and Python scripts.

research premise

Semi-Automated Expansion of FrameNet

January 2019 - May 2019

Research Assistant

FrameNet is a lexical database with context-aware word entries developed upon the theory of Frame Semantics. I cooperated with three other mentees to expand the Berkeley FrameNet with modern vocabulary taken from subtitle corpora. To speed up the process, we used the Python API built by our mentor and experimented with NLP tools such as GloVe and word2vec.

https://framenet.icsi.berkeley.edu

Linguistics

Distinctive Feature Sorter

November 2018 - April 2019

Creator

With the hope to help linguistics students study phonology, I started this project as a command-line Python program that filters the sounds in American English (as IPA objects) by the Chomsky-Halle distinctive features. I launched a web version in the following semester for the final project of a web design class, which won the Staff Pick award. There is an ongoing effort to add more languages to the sorter, and the website is accesible to the public at the link below.

https://dfsorter.github.io
